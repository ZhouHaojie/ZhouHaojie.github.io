Morphological analysis

  • Combination to actionable concepts

    • A morphological box is defined as a two-dimensional matrix solution.
    • The first column of the matrix contains the partial function/realisation of the product. 第一列包含产品的部分功能/实现
    • For each sub-function line by line, the associated solution alternatives are then listed. 列出相关的解决方案
    • The solutions for the sub-functions are illustrated as words, sketches or combinations.

  • Combination of partial solutions

    • Are all partial solutions in the morphological box documented, step by step combining of the partial solutions forms alternatives for an overall concept
    • It does not make sense to consider all theoretical concepts combined.
    • Not every solution alternative of a function can be combined with the partial solutions of other functions.

Basic types of requirement

  • Business Requirements
  • User Requirements

Standards for SRS and SyRS

  • The standards are based on a model that produces a document that helps

    • customers to accurately describe what they wish to obtain
    • suppliers to understand exactly what the customer wants
    • individuals to a accomplish the following goals:
      • develop SRS/SyRS outline for their own organizations
      • develop the format and content of their SRS/SyRS

  • Benefits by using Standards:

    • A simple framework for the organization of the document itself
    • Particularly beneficial to the requirements engineer because it has been widely deployed across a broad range of application domains

Summary of QFD Example

Disadvantages and Advantages

  • Disadvantage of QFD

    • Time and resources consuming
    • Process limitation in iteration support
    • Focus on quality other than functionality

  • Advantage of QFD

    • Customer / User involvement
    • Focus on customer needs
    • Team builder Improve product or service quality
    • Shorter development cycles
    • Lower costs and greater productivity
    • Reduces design changes
    • Documentation requirements
    • Good for communication, decision making and planning
    • Allows for a lot of information in a small space
