Lifecycle Models-Phase Models

发表于 2020-01-08 更新于 2020-01-21 分类于 Systems and Software Engineering ， Lifecycle Models 阅读次数：

Phase Models

What is a phase model?
sequence of working tasks(design steps, phase concept)

  • according activities
  • definition of expected results
  • criteria for completion of tasks
  • According budget and schedule

SDLC: System Development Life Cycle

Describes a process for planning, creating, testing, maintenance and deploying a system. The system can be composed of hardware only, software only, or a combination of both

Waterfall Model

Pros:

  • simple and easy to understand and use
  • easy to manage
  • works well for smaller projects

Cons:

  • No working software is produced until late during the life cycle
  • High amounts of risk and uncertainty
  • Not a good model for complex and object-oriented project

Spiral Model

Spiral Model is a generalization of the waterfall model and is an iterative model.

Initiating and terminating the spiral:

  • Initiating the process

    The spiral gets started by a hypothesis that a particular operational mission could be improved by a software effort.

  • Terminating the process

    The spiral process involves a test of this hypothesis: At any time, if the hypothesis fails the test, the spiral is terminated.

Advantages:

  • Estimates (budget, schedule) become more and more realistic as work progresses.
  • Software engineers can get their hands in and start working on a project earlier.
  • Easier to cope with the changes inherent to software development.

Disadvantages:

  • Highly customized limiting re-usability because different for each application.
  • Estimates of budget and time are harder to judge at the beginning of the project since the requirements evolve through the process.
  • Risk of not meeting initial budget or schedule

Y-Diagram

The Y-Diagram depicts(描绘) the different perspectives in the hardware design(VLSI). Mostly, it is used for the development of IC.

Global transformation: The general model for the designing process is based on a sequence of transformation steps and level (or view) changes with every step.

Local transformation: The design is often optimized or verified on a given level. Since there is no level or view change.

V-Model

The V-Model is an internationally recognized development standard for IT systems.

The Sub-Models

Four sub-models are closely linked to on another and influence each other concerning the exchange of products/results.

PM: Project Management

SD: System Development

QA: Quality Assurance

CM: Configuration Management

Disadvantages:

  • Does not handle iterations
  • Does not easily handle dynamic changes in requirements
  • Does not contain risk analysis activities

Prototyping Model

Naturally, Waterfall and V-Model assume their requirements specification as complete and consistent after their specification phase and are therefore rather strict.

A prototype demonstrates selected characteristic features of the product in practical use

Advantages:

  • Reduced time and costs for first example
  • Speed for first examples Improved and increased user involvement

Disadvantages:

  • Insufficient analysis
  • User confusion of prototype and finished system
  • Excessive development time of the prototype
  • Expense of implementing prototyping

Agile Model(敏捷性模型)

Main points:(敏捷开发宣言内容)

  • Individuals and interactions(个体与交互) >(胜过) processes and tools
  • Working software > comprehensive documentation
  • customer collaboration(客户协作) > contract negotiation(合同谈判)
  • Responding to change > following a plan
Life Cycle

Scrum - Roles(Scrum 团队成员)

Scrum is an agile, lightweight process for managing and controlling software and product development in rapidly changing environments.

敏捷开发是一种软件开发方法，基于迭代和增量开发，通过自组织，跨团队，沟通协作完成开发工作。

  • Scrum Master

    • Represents management to the project and vice versa
    • Typically filed(提交) by a project manager
    • Responsible for enacting scrum values and practices
    • Acts as moderator and coach
    • Guiding and protecting the team

  • Scrum Team:

    • Typically 5-10 people
    • Cross-functional (QA, programmers, UI designers etc.)
    • Team is self-organizing
    • Membership can only change between sprints

  • Product Owner:

    • Acts like one voice
    • Is responsible for product features and backlog
    • Typically a product manager -> knows what needs to be build and in which order
Sprint Meeting(Sprint 会议)

  1. Daily Scrum Meeting (每日站立会议)

    • Is a short (15 min) meeting, which is held dead on time everyday before the Team starts working 会议时长为15分钟，会议应在固定地点和每天的同一时间举行
    • Participants: Scrum Master, Scrum Team
    • Every Team member should answer on 3 questions
      • What did you do since the last scrum? 昨天你完成了那些工作？
      • What are you doing until the next scrum? 今天你打算做什么？
      • What is stopping you getting on with the work? 完成你的目标是否存在什么障碍？（Scrum主管需要记下这些障碍）

  2. Sprint Planning Meeting (Sprint 计划会议)

    • select what work is to be done
    • prepare the Sprint Backlog(需求) that details the time it will take to do that work, with the entire team
    • Identify and communicate how much of the work is likely to be done during the current sprint
    • 8 hours limited
      • (1st 4 hours) Entire team: dialog for prioritizing the product Backlog
      • (2nd 4 hours) Development team: hashing out a plan for the sprint, resulting in the sprint backlog

  3. Sprint Review Meeting (评审会议)

    • Is held at the end of each Sprint 评审会议在每个迭代结束后举行，在会议上团队演示此次迭代中完成了那些工作，一般会有相关的DEMO演示。
    • Business functionality which was created during the Sprint is demonstrated to the Product Owner Informal, should not distract team of doing its work

  4. Pre-Project/Kickoff Meeting

    • A special form of Sprint Planning Meeting
    • Meeting before the begin of the project
User Stories (用户故事)

A short description (some sentences) of what the user of the system does or needs to do as part of his job function. ‘who’, ‘what’, ‘why’ .

Most common approach in agile development is to create user stories and to split them afterwards into tasks

Themes, Epics, User Stories, Tasks

Sometimes the User Story is too big for being delivered in a sprint. To solve this, you have to use the Epic concept.

  • Epic
    • Very big user story (too big for one single sprint)
    • Split into several sub-stories
    • Kept together by a common topic
    • One big feature of a product, which can also impact other products
  • Theme
    • Top of everything
    • Major release in terms of a project
    • One of the cornerstones of the project itself
    • Drive the strategy and express a direction

Scrum Artifacts (文档)
  • Product Backlog
    • Requirements for a system, expressed as a prioritized list of backlog Items (Spreadsheet) 整个系统的需求文档，有优先级的描述(电子表格) / Is not exact粗略
    • Is managed and owned by a Product Owner
    • can be changed and re-prioritized before each PM

  • Estimation of Product Backlog Items(产品订单的估算)

    • Establishes team’s velocity
    • Determining units of complexity(story points, work days/hours)
    • Methods of estimation

  • Sprint Backlog

    • Sprint Backlog is a subset of Product Backlog Items, which defines the work for a Sprint
      • Is created ONLY by Team members
      • Each Item has it’s own status
      • Should be updated every day
    • Sprint Backlog
      • No more then 300 tasks in the list
      • If a task requires more than 16 hours, it should be broken down
      • Team can add or subtract items from the list.
      • Product Owner is not allowed to do it.
      • It’s a FORECAST! It’s a good warning monitor

  • Burn Down Charts(燃尽图)

    A burn down chart is a graphical representation of work left to do versus time 显示当前冲刺中未完成的任务数目

Scrum Board

Advantages:

  • Completely developed and tested features in short iterations
  • Simplicity of the process
  • Clearly defined rules
  • Increasing productivity
  • Self-organizing each team member carries a lot of responsibility improved communication

Drawbacks:

  • “Undisciplined hacking” (no written documentation)
  • Violation of responsibility
  • Current mainly carried by the inventors
  • Focused on SW mainly
